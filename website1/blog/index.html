Why A Pediatric Dentist?

Bringing your child to a pediatric dentist allows your child to be seen by a specialist that solely focuses on treating the young. To be certified as a pediatric dentist, one must complete four years of dental school and an additional two to three years of specialized training. These additional years allows pediatric dentists to increase their knowledge base about treating infants, children and young adults.

Nakamura Children’s Dentistry is concerned about your child's oral and total health care. Therefore, establishing a Dental Home is important as it provides your child a foundation to establish strong preventative dental health habits to keep them free from dental disease. During your visits with us, we are committed to creating a fun, positive and nurturing environment so that your child will have a lifetime of positive experiences going to the dental office.

Preventative Care





Although your child will eventually lose all of their primary (baby) teeth, it is important that these baby teeth are taken care of. The baby teeth are very important for the proper development of the child’s permanent (adult) teeth. They also act as placeholders allowing for the proper eruption and alignment of the adult teeth.

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends that your child be seen at least twice a year for a dental checkup and cleaning. These dental visits allow for the teeth to be examined and cleaned. Removing the plaque which may adhere to the teeth help to prevent cavities and gingivitis. Fluoride treatments also allow for the teeth to be strengthened as fluoride will be incorporated into the enamel.

Preventative care is also important as it allows us to evaluate your child’s brushing and flossing habits and to provide additional support and instruction to help keep the mouth healthy and clean. Baby teeth have thinner and weaker enamel than adult teeth, so it is important to continue to return for your child’s 6 month checkup and cleaning.

At these visits, your child may need to take X-ray to diagnose any tooth decay or abnormalities. These X-ray will only be taken when necessary to ensure that your child has good oral health. Additionally, your child’s teeth will also be evaluated for placement of sealants to prevent cavities and potential orthodontic problems.

Here at Nakamura Children’s Dentistry we can monitor your child’s dental health and provide routine preventative care which can decrease your child’s chances of developing cavities or gingivitis. Please give us a call to schedule an appointment!

First Dental Visit

Bringing your child in for their first dental visit is a big milestone. We recommend that your child be seen for their first visit by their first birthday or when their first baby tooth begins to erupt from their gums. By coming into our office early we can monitor the child’s overall dental health/development and establish a proper preventative care program. These preventative programs are important as Early Childhood Caries (baby bottle tooth decay or nursing cavities) can start once a child’s diet include anything besides breast milk.

At the first visit, we will exam your child’s teeth and also discuss brushing, flossing and the impact of your child's diet on their oral health. This visit will also allow you to ask any questions you may have pertaining to your child’s oral health including when to expect the other baby teeth to erupt, strategies for teething and need for fluoride supplementation.
