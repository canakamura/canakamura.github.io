Children’s Dentist in Bangor

May 6, 2016

It is important that everyone have their teeth checked at least twice a year for routine checkups and exams. This is especially important for children who are just developing teeth and whose teeth need to be monitored as they continue to grow. If you are in need of a children’s dentist in Bangor and you do not need to look any further than Chris Nakamura’s Dentistry. Are pediatric dentist can help your child develop a strong healthy teeth by preventing pediatric dental decay and teaching in the values of good oral hygiene.

It is generally recommended that people of all ages and all states of dental health visit their local dentist for checkups and exams about once every six months or so. Some people may only reserve dental visits for emergencies and other similar situations of the like, and while it is still very important that you see your dentist in such an event, routine dental visits can actually prevent the need for emergency visits at all. This is especially important for children whose teeth are first emerging and need regular care. Ideally, your child should be into see the dentist once there first teeth begin to bud. From there our children’s dentist in Bangor can properly monitor their overall dental health and development. Even though baby teeth are not permanent they do act as important placeholders for the permanent teeth that will one day take their place. Baby teeth are rather small and sensitive, which means that they are especially prone to rot and decay. This can be difficult to prevent and children who are still learning how to take care of their dental health on their own and are notorious for loving cavity causing sweets and candies. With biannual visits here with our pediatric dentist at Chris Nakamura’s Dentistry your child can get the comprehensive dental cleanings and other forms of preventative care that they need to ward off the likelihood of decay and disease.

Is also important that you visit us here at Chris Nakamura’s Dentistry if your child begins to exhibit any signs or symptoms related to cavities or decay such as oral pain, foul smelling breath, or even gum inflammation. If you are in need of a children’s dentist in Bangor simply call us here at our dental office to learn more and to schedule your appointment today.
